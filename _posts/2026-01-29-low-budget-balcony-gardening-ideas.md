Below are 10 low budget balcony gardening ideas that are beginner-friendly and perfect for small homes.

1. Start with Easy and Low-Maintenance Plants

![Easy Balcony Plants]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/1-easy-balcony-plants.jpg)

Begin with plants that grow easily and donâ€™t need much care, such as money plant, tulsi, mint, aloe vera, and snake plant.

2. Use Recycled Containers as Pots

![Recycled Containers for Balcony Gardening]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/2-recycled-containers.jpg)

Old buckets, plastic bottles, paint containers, and tin boxes work perfectly as plant pots. Just add drainage holes at the bottom.

3. Make Your Own Soil Mix

![DIY Soil Mix for Balcony Garden]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/3-soil-mix.jpg)

Mix garden soil, sand, and homemade compost to create a healthy and low-cost soil mix for balcony plants.

4. Grow Herbs for Daily Use

![Balcony Herb Garden]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/4-herb-garden.jpg)

Herbs like coriander, mint, and curry leaves grow well in small containers and save money on groceries.

5. Use Vertical Space Smartly

![Vertical Balcony Gardening]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/5-vertical-garden.jpg)

Use vertical stands, wall planters, or hanging pots to grow more plants without using floor space.

6. Reuse Household Water

![Water Saving Balcony Gardening]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/6-water-saving.jpg)

Reuse RO waste water or leftover drinking water to reduce water usage and keep your garden eco-friendly.

7. Choose Flowering Plants for Color

![Balcony Flower Plants]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/7-flowering-plants.jpg)

Plants like marigold and portulaca add color and beauty to your balcony without much expense.

8. Compost Kitchen Waste at Home

![Homemade Compost for Plants]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/8-homemade-compost.jpg)

Vegetable peels, tea leaves, and dry leaves can be used to make compost at home, reducing waste and cost.

9. Arrange Plants for Proper Sunlight

![Balcony Sunlight for Plants]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/9-balcony-sunlight.jpg)

Place sun-loving plants near railings and shade-loving plants in corners to ensure healthy growth.

10. Maintain Plants with Minimal Effort

![Balcony Plant Maintenance]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gardning/10-plant-care.jpg)

Water plants regularly, remove dry leaves, and check soil moisture. Just a few minutes daily is enough.

Final Thoughts

Balcony gardening on a low budget is simple, relaxing, and rewarding. With recycled containers, easy plants, and homemade compost, you can create a fresh and green balcony without spending much money.
